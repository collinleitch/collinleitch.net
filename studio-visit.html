Studio Visit with Julia

February 4th 2022





These are all works I made in the last year after coming back from a show/residency in Peru.
I have been calling these pieces 'relief drawings' as they almost always start as a drawing.
After isolating each constituative shape as a vector drawing, I use them to make models for 3D printing.
These prints are then mounted on mahogany hardwood CNC-milled to match the outer profile of the drawing.
Even from my previous work, it has always been important for me to treat the 3D printer as non-illusionistically as possible.
This often means that rather than thinking about it as a means to deliver fully-formed, representative 3D models (figures, toys, tools, etc) into physical space,
I prefer to treat it as a tool that performs a single function: precisely depositing plastic via a set of spatial coordinates.
